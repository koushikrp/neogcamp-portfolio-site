What is the Potential of Blockchain and why should we acknowledge it?

August, 2021 * Explained about Blockchain using understanding from online resources in layman terms.

What is Blockchain?

Blockchain technology, in simple words, is an innovative way to get many different persons/investors to create one common version of something leveraging the power of internet. Single versions of things are important for uses like finding out if Ram paid Hari and only Hari for that Pen (and whether he didn’t copy his money and also pay someone else).

Why is it invaluable?

Actually before blockchain, there was no way to get multiple persons to create something in a unique way, and so we would usually have to put our trust in one person or a centralized entity (e.g. a bank) to create and maintain the unique versions of things we needed. Trusting one centralized organization is very costly as they can then charge us money to do things at prices that may not be fair. They can also manipulate the thing that we’ve trusted them with to suit their dreams and goals, potentially in a way that hurts everyone else. This old way of doing things is also very inefficient since different groups of people would choose different representatives to maintain unique versions of the things important to them, making it very hard to interact between different groups.

When you send Rs.50 using Paytm to your friend in Hyderabad, Paytm isn't literally putting that Rs.50 on an airplane. When you received the money it doesn’t mean that the cash just came out in an airplane and was just deposited into a safe box with your name on it.

When someone trades stocks using any software, the stock exchange is not using paper stock certificates for the transactions and your bank isn't physically moving paper bills/receipts. All they really are, are giant digital LEDGERS moving digital numbers(unique numbers) around from one account to the other in a secure way.

Hence all your money, stocks and even identity are just information in ledgers i.e. Data. Does it really matter if a stock certificate is grey or red or 1 inch larger? The container of the data (like money certificates, bonds, other paper work) doesn’t matter. What matters is that, which is being contained. Value, Identity and Ownership are those are things that exist in the mind. And those things are recorded in our record books(offline ledgers).

Conventionally, you would have had a central authority moderating a record book. In what other way would you do it? An accountant cannot freely share his books to everyone. If everyone had full access to it, I would just add 5 crores to my balance and retire (although someone else could then take it away from me).

Final Words

So we have presiding institutions(central entities) by necessity. The paytm ledger is stored in a paytm server and lives in the paytm network. The bank ledger lives in a bank computer in a remote location. That ledger is always updated, secured and perfectly managed centrally. We trust these organizations to keep the integrity of the ledger. Now when there is no central authority, by some magic technology (blockchain), we can still have 100% Integrity. Nobody can cheat the system. So, investing in such technology at the expense of changing the entire ecosystem seems to be a right choice. It may be slow pace now, but it will accelerate.
